# Sample questions to ask Kairos

Things to actually ask, grouped by how the skill will classify them. Horary and
electional need no birth data at all (just your city), which makes them the
right openers for a first session.

## Horary (yes/no on a real matter)

Cast for the moment you ask. The house in parentheses is the matter's house the
skill will pick.

- "Will I get the job I interviewed for last week?" (10th)
- "Will the money from the invoice actually come through this month?" (2nd)
- "Is this deal going to close?" (7th)
- "Will we get back together?" (7th; the example gallery's honest "no" is this question)
- "Will my apartment application be approved?" (4th)
- "Will my visa get approved in time for the trip?" (9th)
- "Should I trust this business partner?" (7th)

## Turned-chart horary (about someone else)

These exercise the derived-house machinery (`querentHouse`):

- "Will my partner win their lawsuit?" (partner = 7th; their lawsuit = 9th-from-7th = radix 3rd)
- "Will my friend get the job they're interviewing for?" (friend = 11th; their career = 10th-from-11th = radix 8th)
- "Will my kid pass the entrance exam?" (child = 5th; their study = 9th-from-5th = radix 1st)

## Electional (find the best moment in a window)

- "When should I launch the product? Best day in the next two weeks."
- "Pick a good time this month to sign the lease."
- "What's the best morning next week to send the fundraising email?"

## Transit (the only kind that asks for your birth data)

- "Is now the right time for me to switch jobs?"
- "Is this a good year for me to move cities?" (pairs well with relocation)
- "Should I start something new this quarter, or wait?"

## The reproducible demo

For a screenshot anyone can verify: "Will I get the job?" asked in London on
2026-06-08 at 11:15 always returns `favorable` / `medium` / `+38`. The full
two-layer reading is in [the worked example](example.md), and the skill offers
this demo on a fresh install (demo computes are never journaled, so trying it
does not touch your real track record).

A practical note: questions with concrete stakes and near-term resolution
dates (job offers, deals, applications) are the most satisfying first
questions, because the calibration loop comes back and asks what happened
within weeks.
